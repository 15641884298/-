Restrictions on Personal Information Processing

The principle of legality    informed consent   Minimize processing    



    Principle of legality: Companies must follow the principles of legality, legitimacy, and necessity when collecting and using personal information. This means that companies cannot collect personal information unrelated to their business at will, and the purpose of collecting information should be clear and communicated to the information subject.

Informed consent: Before processing personal information, companies usually need to obtain explicit consent from individuals, unless otherwise specified by law (such as information necessary to fulfill a labor contract). This requires companies to clearly explain to individuals the purpose, scope, and method of collecting information before collecting it.

Minimization processing: Companies can only collect personal information directly related to the services they provide, and should limit it to the minimum scope necessary to achieve the processing purpose. This prevents excessive collection of personal information.
